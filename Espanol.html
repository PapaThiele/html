"Regrets, I've had a few...But then again, too few to mention" Frank Sinatra "My Way"

One of my biggest regrets was not listening to my Mom. I don't just mean her advice, wisdom and direction but actually listening to her talk. My Mom was multi-lingual. She was fluent in English and Spanish and even knew a little bit of Navajo. She would use Spanish when she was mad or when the English word just didn't seem to convey the right meaning. She used a mix of English and Spanish when talking to the cats we had as pets growing up. It wasn't that I was too cool to learn all but a handful of words in Spanish, I just didn't see the value at the time. Now that she is gone, I have missed my chance to learn in the easiest way...simple conversation with someone you know. My children were 6 and almost 4 when she died and they do not remember the one time they met her. 2 years ago we adopted 2 kittens. Subconsciously, some f the Spanish came back to me. Along with cat stories I share with them, I try to tell them the words or phrases in Spanish she would use. It is a way to keep her memory alive and maybe interest my kids in a foreign language.
